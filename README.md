# 📄 PDF OCR Converter — Built for Law Students
A free, browser-based tool that converts scanned and image-only PDFs into fully searchable, OCR-compliant documents — built by a law student, for law students.
Why this exists:
Legal education runs on PDFs. Case law, bare acts, judgment copies, study materials — almost all of it is distributed as scanned documents that can't be searched, highlighted, or navigated efficiently. As an NLS student coming from an engineering and data science background, I kept running into this problem and decided to build a solution rather than work around it.
About the builder:
I'm Rakshit Dwaram, currently in my second year of LL.B. at the National Law School of India University (NLSIU), Bangalore. Before law, I worked as a data scientist with a background in engineering. This tool is the first of what I hope will be several practical intersections of my technical background and legal education — building things that actually solve problems students face every day.
Who it's for:
Any law student who has ever tried to Ctrl+F a scanned judgment and failed. It works on any PDF — bare acts, case compilations, reading packets, court documents — and gives you back a version you can actually work with: searchable, selectable, and screen-reader friendly.
It's completely free because access to usable study materials shouldn't be a technical barrier.


## ✨ Features

- Upload any scanned or image-only PDF
- Auto-deskew tilted scans
- Clean noise before OCR
- Multi-language support (English, French, German, Spanish, Japanese, Arabic, Hindi, Chinese, and more)
- Configurable output optimisation (file size vs quality)
- Skip pages that already have text
- Download the OCR'd PDF instantly
